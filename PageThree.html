Poison Dart Frog

These frogs are considered one of Earth's most toxic species.

  • Unlike many other amphibians, poison dart frogs are diurnal, meaning they’re most active in the daytime – just like humans.
  • Poison dart frogs are primarily terrestrial animals, meaning
    that they spend most of their time on the forest floor, where
    they hunt for ants, termites and mites.
  • Poison dart frogs are aposematic animals, which means
    that their colouring is a warning to predators to say, “don’t
    eat me, I’m poisonous”.
